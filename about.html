About The Shop

Since the chair was new Lynn Road · Gaywood

A small, busy barbershop a few minutes' walk from the Queen Elizabeth Hospital — quietly looking after the lads of Gaywood and King's Lynn.

The chair

It's the same place it always was.

Clock Barber sits on Lynn Road in Gaywood — the bit of King's Lynn that's always running errands. Mums on the school run, lads getting fresh for Friday, dads who haven't had a proper trim since the last cup final.

The shop is unfussy. Two chairs, a queue if you're unlucky, and a wall clock that's been wrong by three minutes for as long as anyone can remember.

If you're new in, you don't need to book. Open the door, ask who's last, take a seat.

What we do

Three things, done properly.

Haircuts. Clipper, scissor, fades, skin fades, taper, crop — whatever the lads in the school playground are asking for this term. Children's cuts welcome any day.

Beards. Trim, shape and line-up. We talk about what you actually want before the clippers come out.

Head shaves. Hot towel, clean finish. Worth booking when we're busy on a Saturday morning — give us a ring.

Local

From the Queen Elizabeth to the Mariners.

We're a minute from the Queen Elizabeth Hospital and five minutes from King's Lynn town centre. Plenty of street parking on Lynn Road. The 36 bus stops nearly outside the door.

If you're coming from town, head out along Gaywood Road — we're on the right after the roundabout, in the little parade.
